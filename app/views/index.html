A verification report is a new kind of pre-sentence advice. It can be used for lower complexity cases that are suitable for electronic monitoring curfew, unpaid work sentences, fines or a combination of these. This allows probation practitioners to be proactive in identifying suitable cases and will reduce the need for unnecessary adjournments.

There are a number of steps to be taken between considering suitability to completing the verification report itself.

1. Check the case meets the criteria for a verification report

You must confirm a defendant's suitability for either a verification report, a pre-sentence report or no advice. This is done though reviewing and gathering information.

Initial review of existing information

A defendant can be considered for a verification report if:

  • their most serious offence is in scope for a community order but not severe enough for a custodial sentence
  • they have been identified as a guilty anticipated plea (GAP)
  • they have no active registrations in Prepare a case for sentence or NDelius (other than low risk of serious harm)
  • there are no case markers in Common Platform
  • they are at liberty on the day of their plea hearing
  • there are no current domestic violence or safeguarding concerns
Information gathering and third party checks

When the defendant meets the criteria for a verification report, further information must be requested to confirm their suitability including:

  • domestic violence checks from the police
  • child safeguarding checks from social services
  • relevant information from other services such as treatment providers
  • relevant information from Youth Justice Services (defendants aged 18-21)
  • an update from their probation practitioner (if they are a current case)
Practitioner review

The practitioner should review the information gathered and third party checks alongside any relevant additional information including:

  • previous risk assessments
  • all risk registrations (active and inactive)
  • probation history
  • CPS pack
  • any relevant PSRs
  • ongoing court cases

If no risk concerns are identified following these checks, a screening interview will be arranged with the defendant.

2. Arrange and hold the screening interview

The screening interview is an opportunity to:

  • discuss and validate information already collated such as accommodation, employment and health
  • gather further evidence to inform and confirm the contents of the verification report
  • consider any complexities or rehabilitative needs linked to race, age or sex through completion of the diversity and inclusion form (DIF)
  • use professional curiosity and judgement when considering how the person has presented

The defendant should be informed in advance via letter or telephone, of the screening interview to provide any paperwork or evidence to verify their current circumstances. A telephone call gives an opportunity for information about the defendant's residency to be gathered which will enable checks for an electronic monitoring curfew to be made.

If concerns arise during the screening interview that would suggest a full assessment is required due to rehabilitative or risk needs, the verification report should be completed with the information you have gathered, noting the concerns that require further assessment. Any new risks identified as a result of the screening interview need to be reviewed against existing risk registrations in NDelius, making any updates as required.

Read the screening interview checklist (PDF, 141KB)

3. Complete the verification report

You can complete the verification report in any order, over a period of time, adding information as you get it.

If you are still waiting on checks coming back you should save a draft and include the missing information when you get it.

In order to edit the verification report, you will need to have Microsoft Word installed on your device and select 'Open in Word desktop app'.

Download editable verification report (Word, 48KB)

4. Share the verification report

You should upload the verification report to Common Platform as soon as it is ready. The court will not need to adjourn for a pre-sentence report to be prepared.
